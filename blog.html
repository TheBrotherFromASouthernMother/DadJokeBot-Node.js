Ghana, The Door of No Return

I was dreading it from the moment we made port. Being a black person in America, it's fairly common to ask where you came from before 1863. Of course, all roads lead back to "Africa", but pointing at a continent made up of 53 nations spread across 12 million square miles is a far cry from specific. Before I began my Semester at Sea voyage, I spoke about it all with my parents. We sat down and retraced our steps. It remained obvious, we would never know the real details, the legacy of colonialism had erased those long ago. However, there was one thing that was for certain. Our roots, well our roots were in Ghana....

South Africa: Truth and Reconciliation

On SAS I learned about something called restorative justice. It's a controversial idea, and though many claim it in principle, few have the strength to claim it in action. In it's simplest definition, restorative justice is the idea that righting wrongs is not solely about arbitration, but rather about justice being a dialogue. It holds that mending the relationship between victim and abuser is as important as handing out sentencing. Restorative justice is about forgiveness, and it is about truth.
